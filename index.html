Our Story

In the Nyumanzi and Ayilo Refugee Settlements of Northern Uganda, thousands of South Sudanese refugees face daily challenges in securing food for their families. Despite their agricultural knowledge and determination, they lack the essential resources needed to cultivate the land.

"Refugees have the will to farm ‚Äî but not the means. PuurKu exists to change that."

PuurKu means "to grow together" in Dinka, reflecting our core belief that sustainable development happens when communities unite around shared goals. Founded by Ajang Chol Aguer, our initiative transforms agricultural challenges into opportunities for empowerment and self-reliance.

The Refugee Agricultural Empowerment Center

Our vision extends beyond immediate needs. We're building a sustainable hub for training, seeds, and farming innovation ‚Äî a place where knowledge flows freely, where the next generation of agricultural leaders is nurtured, and where hope takes root in fertile soil.

This center will serve as a beacon of self-reliance, demonstrating that with the right support, refugee communities can not only survive but thrive, contributing to the broader agricultural landscape of Northern Uganda.
